Turn 1 · Brand & visual direction

Three ways to say "points are money"

Each direction is a full point of view — name, palette (light + dark), type pairing, and how it survives an arbitrary tenant color — shown on the two screens that decide everything: the earn celebration (the emotional peak) and the outlet dashboard (the customer's home). None use the old stamp/cream language. Reply with an id (e.g. 1a) to pick one; I'll build the full token system + component specs + every surface on it next. Mixing is fine — "1a's palette, 1c's numerals."

1a
Stampd.
"Every rupee, remembered."

A calm fintech passbook. Points read like a bank balance — big tabular monospace figures, credit-green for value gained, a rational cool-neutral canvas. Trustworthy, legible on a cheap screen, quietly premium. The ledger is the star.

1b
Sona
"Turn spending into gold."

Sona means gold. Points are treated as something precious and earned — warm, editorial, human. A vivid marigold accent (a nod to Nepal without cliché) over rich espresso-ink in dark mode and neutral warm ivory in light. Serif display gives it craft; it feels like belonging, not a database.

1c
Mohar
"Money that moves."

A mohar is a historic Nepali coin. High-energy and kinetic — near-black canvas, one electric accent, oversized geometric numerals built to animate. Designed around the earn/redeem moment: the number is the show. Young, confident, unmistakably a currency you spend.
